Heritage

The Ancient Art of
Bilona Ghee Making

A time-honoured process passed down through generations at Deshinge Dairy Farm. Every step is intentional, every detail matters.

01

Milk Collection

Fresh A2 milk is collected daily from our free-grazing Gir cows. The milk is never stored for long — freshness is paramount.

02

Curd Formation

The warm milk is naturally set into curd using a traditional starter culture. This fermentation develops beneficial probiotics.

03

Hand Churning

The curd is hand-churned using a wooden churner (Bilona) in a traditional rhythmic motion — separating butter from buttermilk.

04

Butter Extraction

Fresh white butter (Makkhan) floats to the top. It is carefully collected — pure, soft, and full of cream.

05

Slow Heating

The butter is slowly heated on low flame. The gradual process caramelises milk solids, creating the signature golden colour and nutty aroma.

06

Handcrafted Ghee

Once the ghee reaches the perfect golden stage, it is strained and cooled. The result: pure, granular, aromatic Desi Ghee.

07

Pure & Packed

The ghee is poured into glass bottles while still warm, preserving its freshness and natural goodness. Ready to reach your kitchen.

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked
Questions

Our ghee is made at Deshinge Dairy Farm using the traditional Bilona method. Fresh A2 milk from Gir cows is set into curd, then hand-churned using a wooden churner to extract butter. This butter is slowly heated on low flame until it transforms into golden, aromatic ghee. The entire process is done in small batches to ensure the highest quality.

Bilona is an ancient Indian method of making ghee. Instead of directly boiling cream, the milk is first cultured into curd, then churned (bilona means "to churn") to separate butter. This butter is then slowly cooked to produce ghee. This method preserves more nutrients and creates the signature granular texture and rich aroma.

Deshinge Ghee has a shelf life of 12 months from the date of manufacturing. Since it contains no water and is prepared using the traditional method, it stays fresh for a long time when stored properly.

Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Always use a clean, dry spoon. Refrigeration is not necessary but can be done. Avoid introducing water or moisture into the jar, as this can reduce shelf life.

Absolutely pure. Deshinge Dairy Farm ghee is independently lab-tested and certified to be free from glyphosate, pesticides, GMOs, and adulterants. The granular texture, golden colour, and rich nutty aroma are hallmarks of genuine Bilona ghee.

A2 milk contains only the A2 beta-casein protein, which is easier to digest than the A1 protein found in regular milk. Gir cows are naturally A2-producing breeds. Studies suggest A2 milk may be gentler on the stomach and less likely to cause digestive discomfort.
